## 1. NC State Programs — Free Counseling & Support

### NC Small Business and Technology Development Center (SBTDC)

- **What:** Free, confidential one-on-one business counseling for NC small businesses
- **Services:** Strategic planning, financial analysis, market research, international business development, government contracting assistance
- **Structure:** 16 offices across the state, affiliated with UNC System campuses
- **Cost:** FREE
- **Website:** https://sbtdc.org
- **Relevance to Adverse:** Free strategic planning help, financial projections for growth, and access to graduate student teams for projects

### NC Small Business Center Network

- **What:** 58 centers located at community colleges across NC
- **Services:** Free seminars, counseling, resources, and networking for small business owners and entrepreneurs
- **Cost:** FREE
- **Relevance:** Local training on marketing, finances, hiring — good for building management skills as you scale

### SCORE Charlotte

- **What:** Free, confidential mentoring from experienced business executives (retired and working)
- **Services:** One-on-one mentoring on business planning, financing, marketing, HR, operations
- **Cost:** FREE
- **Website:** https://score.org/charlotte/
- **Relevance:** Get paired with a mentor who's scaled a services business. Advice on pricing, sales process, hiring your first employee

## 2. Grants & Incentive Funding

### One North Carolina Small Business Program

- **What:** State-funded grants for technology companies pursuing federal SBIR/STTR awards
- **Two components:**
  - **Incentive Funds:** Reimburses up to 50% of costs to prepare/submit SBIR/STTR Phase I proposals (max ~$3,000 per grant, $200K pool in FY2026)
  - **Matching Funds:** Grants to NC businesses that have already won a federal Phase I SBIR/STTR award ($1.87M available in FY2026)
- **Administered by:** NC Department of Commerce
- **Website:** https://www.commerce.nc.gov/grants-incentives/technology-funds/one-north-carolina-small-business-program
- **Relevance to Adverse:** If you develop proprietary technology (AI lead scoring, automation tools), you could pursue SBIR Phase I and get matching state funds. Worth exploring if you productize your pipeline tech.

### Charlotte Business Matching Grants

- **What:** City of Charlotte provides matching grants to help small businesses grow
- **Website:** https://www.charlottenc.gov/Growth-and-Development/Economic-Development/Public-Private-Partnerships/Business-Matching-Grants
- **Relevance:** Direct funding support for growth initiatives

### Beyond Open CLT

- **What:** Competitive small business grant program for diverse-owned businesses (minority, woman, veteran, LGBTQ, persons with disabilities)
- **Focus:** Businesses in Charlotte's Corridors of Opportunity
- **Website:** https://www.beyondopenclt.com/
- **Relevance:** Adam qualifies as a minority business owner. Worth applying.

### CSBDF Charlotte Small Business Ecosystem Grant

- **What:** Customized technical assistance courses + grant for Charlotte-based entrepreneurs
- **Provider:** Carolina Small Business Development Fund
- **Website:** https://www.carolinasmallbusiness.org/charlotte
- **Relevance:** Technical assistance + funding. Direct applicability.

## 3. Certifications (State & Local)

### NC HUB Certification (Historically Underutilized Business)

- **What:** Statewide certification for minority-, women-, and disadvantaged-owned businesses
- **Eligible groups:** Black, Hispanic, Asian American, American Indian, Female, Disabled, or Disadvantaged (51%+ ownership required)
- **Benefits:**
  - Listed in state HUB database used by all state agencies for procurement
  - State agencies have goals/targets for HUB participation
  - Gateway to other certifications (CBI, NCDOT)
- **Administered by:** NC Department of Administration, HUB Office
- **Website:** https://www.doa.nc.gov/divisions/historically-underutilized-businesses-hub
- **Cost:** FREE to apply
- **Relevance:** Adam qualifies. This is foundational — opens doors to state contracts and other programs.

### Charlotte Business INClusion (CBI) — SBE Certification

- **What:** City of Charlotte certification for Small Business Enterprises (SBE), plus registration for MBE/WBE firms that are already HUB-certified
- **Benefits:**
  - Listed in city's Supplier Diversity Management System (SDMS)
  - Access to city contracting opportunities
  - Free monthly workshops
  - Organizational training and strategic development
  - Networking opportunities with city departments and prime contractors
  - Partial sponsorships for annual membership dues to business organizations
- **Requirements:** Local small business
- **Website:** https://www.charlottenc.gov/small-business
- **Cost:** FREE
- **Relevance:** City of Charlotte spends significantly on IT, web, and digital services. Getting certified puts Adverse on the radar.

### NCDOT MBE/WBE Certification

- **What:** NC Department of Transportation certification for minority/women business enterprises
- **Benefits:** Access to NCDOT contracts and subcontracting opportunities (NCDOT is one of the largest purchasers in the state)
- **Website:** https://connect.ncdot.gov/business/SmallBusiness/Pages/MBE%20WBE%20Certification%20Process.aspx
- **Relevance:** Less directly relevant for digital services unless NCDOT has IT project needs

## 4. Federal Programs via State Channels

### SBA 8(a) Business Development Program

- **What:** 9-year federal program providing contracting preferences and business development support to socially/economically disadvantaged small businesses
- **Benefits:**
  - Sole-source federal contracts (up to $4.5M for services)
  - Set-aside contract competitions (only 8(a) firms can bid)
  - Mentorship, training, and counseling
  - Joint ventures with established firms
  - Federal agencies have 5% spending goal for 8(a) firms
- **Eligibility (post-June 2026 reform):**
  - Must be a small business (per SBA size standards)
  - Owner must be unconditionally in control
  - Must have been in business at least 2 years
  - Owner must demonstrate individual social disadvantage (discrimination/bias experienced)
  - Owner must demonstrate economic disadvantage (net worth < $850K excluding home/business)
  - Good character
- **⚠️ Recent Change (June 11, 2026):** SBA removed race-based presumption of social disadvantage. Applicants now must individually prove they experienced discrimination that caused material harm. This applies to individually-owned firms. Entity-owned firms (tribal, ANC, NHO, CDC) are not affected.
- **What this means for Adam:** Still eligible, but requires a personal narrative documenting specific instances of discrimination/bias rather than relying on group membership alone.
- **Website:** https://sba.gov/8a
- **Relevance:** HIGH. If approved, access to sole-source federal IT/web contracts worth millions. The application is intensive but the payoff over 9 years is enormous.

### SBA Small Business Development Centers (SBDCs)

- **What:** Free one-on-one counseling and low-cost training (the SBTDC *is* NC's SBDC network)
- **Services:** Procurement and contracting, market research, 8(a) program support, business planning
- **Website:** https://www.sba.gov/local-assistance/resource-partners/score-business-mentoring

### SBIR/STTR Programs (Federal)

- **What:** Federal grants for small businesses doing R&D
- **Phase I:** ~$150K-$275K for feasibility/proof of concept (6-12 months)
- **Phase II:** ~$750K-$1.5M for full R&D (2 years)
- **Agencies:** DoD, NSF, NIH, DOE, NASA, etc.
- **⚠️ Status Note (2026):** SBIR/STTR program lapsed Oct 1, 2025 due to congressional disagreement. Monitor for reauthorization.
- **Relevance:** If Adverse develops novel AI/automation tech, this is non-dilutive R&D funding. Combine with NC's One NC matching funds for maximum leverage.

## 5. Minority/Disadvantaged Business Programs

### Mecklenburg County Business Diversity & Inclusion (BDI)

- **What:** County program ensuring minorities, women, and small businesses have equal opportunity in county contracts
- **Services:** Helps firms grow and become competitive for governmental purchasing
- **Website:** https://oed.mecknc.gov/BDI
- **Relevance:** County contracts for IT/web services

### Charlotte Contractor Development Program

- **What:** Comprehensive program for small, minority, and women-owned firms
- **Services:**
  - Technical assistance
  - Business development guidance
  - Financial guidance
  - Bonding assistance
  - Capacity building
- **Eligibility:** City MWSBE and NCDOT certified DBE firms
- **Website:** https://www.charlottenc.gov/Growth-and-Development/Doing-Business/CBI/Contractor-Development-Program
- **Relevance:** Business development resources to grow capacity for larger contracts

### NC S651 — The HUSTLE Act (Pending Legislation)

- **What:** Establishes the NC Small Business Capital Access Program with specific MWBE components
- **Key provisions:**
  - **Small Business Loan Guarantee Program:** Guarantees loans from qualified lenders — up to 80% coverage for small businesses, up to 90% for MWBEs
  - **MWBE Growth Fund:** Direct investments to qualified MWBEs ranging from $250,000 to $2 million
  - **Office of Small Business Capital Access** within NC Dept of Commerce
  - **11-member oversight board**
- **Status:** Filed in 2025 session, progressing through legislature
- **Relevance:** VERY HIGH if passed. $250K-$2M direct investment potential for a certified MWBE like Adverse.

## 6. Government Contracting Opportunities

### SBTDC Government Contracting Assistance Program (GCAP)

- **What:** Free counseling to help NC small businesses compete for government contracts (federal, state, local)
- **Services:**
  - One-on-one counseling on contract strategy
  - SAM.gov registration assistance
  - Bid/proposal preparation guidance
  - Data analytics for aligning with procurement trends
  - Connections to prime contractors seeking subcontractors
- **Website:** https://sbtdc.org/programs/gcap
- **Cost:** FREE
- **Relevance:** CRITICAL. They can help Adverse position for IT/web services contracts with government entities.

### NC E-Procurement System

- **What:** Electronic procurement system for state agencies, community colleges, and universities
- **How it works:** State puts out bids electronically; registered vendors receive notifications and can submit proposals
- **Governed by:** NC Division of Purchase and Contract
- **Website:** https://ncadmin.nc.gov/businesses/vendor-resources/nc-bids-vendors
- **Relevance:** Register as a vendor to receive IT contract notifications from NC state entities

### State Term Contract 920S — IT Specialty Services

- **What:** State contract vehicle for IT specialty services providers
- **Who buys:** NC state agencies, community colleges, universities, some local government
- **Services covered:** IT consulting, web development, software, digital services
- **Relevance:** VERY HIGH. If Adverse gets on this contract, state entities can buy web/digital services directly from you.

### Federal Contracting (SAM.gov)

- **What:** Register in System for Award Management to be eligible for federal contracts
- **Website:** https://sam.gov
- **Cost:** FREE
- **Relevance:** Required first step for any federal contract, including 8(a) set-asides

## 9. Capital Access & Loans

### NC Small Business Capital Access Program (NC-CAP)

- **What:** Loan loss reserve program encouraging financial institutions to make small business loans
- **How it works:** Banks can draw on a guarantee pool in case of defaults, reducing their risk in lending to small businesses
- **History:** Has helped leverage $800M+ in credit for NC small businesses since 1994

### SBA Loans (via NC District Office)

- **What:** SBA guarantees loans made by participating lenders
- **Types:**
  - **7(a):** General purpose loans up to $5M
  - **504:** Fixed-rate financing for major assets (equipment, real estate)
  - **Microloans:** Up to $50K for startups/small businesses
- **NC District Office:** https://sba.gov/district/North-Carolina
- **Relevance:** If you need capital for equipment, hiring, or expansion. SBA guarantee makes banks more willing to lend to newer businesses.

### S651 MWBE Growth Fund (Pending)

- **What:** Direct investments $250K-$2M to qualified MWBEs
- **Status:** Legislative bill in progress (2025-2026 session)
- **Relevance:** Watch this closely. If passed, significant capital available.

## Notes

- **HUB certification is the foundation.** Get this first — it unlocks CBI, NCDOT, and positions you for 8(a).
- **The 8(a) program is the biggest potential play** for a minority-owned digital services company. Sole-source contracts up to $4.5M for services. But the June 2026 rule change means you need to document specific discrimination experiences rather than rely on group-based presumption.
- **Government contracting for IT/web services is massive in NC.** State agencies, UNC System (16 universities), 58 community colleges — all need websites, digital services, and IT consulting.
- **Most of these programs are completely free.** The main investment is your time in applications and meetings.
- **Charlotte is actively investing in its small business ecosystem** with the 2026 implementation plan from the Ecosystem Assessment. Get plugged in now while programs are being designed.
