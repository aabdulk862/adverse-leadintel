## Move 3: SBA 8(a) Business Development Program

**What it is:** A 9-year federal program that gives you access to sole-source contracts up to $4.5M (services), set-aside competitions, mentorship, and business development support.

**Why it matters:**

- **Mentor-Protégé** — partner with large primes, use their past performance
- **9 years** of preferential access to the federal marketplace
- This is the single highest-ROI certification for a minority-owned tech services firm

**Timeline:** 90-180 days for approval. Prep work takes 4-8 weeks.

### Step 3.4: Write the Social Disadvantage Narrative

**This is the most important part of the application under the new rules.**

The narrative must demonstrate that Adam has personally experienced discrimination or bias based on racial, ethnic, or cultural identity, and that it caused material harm.

**Structure your narrative like this:**

```
1. IDENTITY (1 paragraph)
   - State your racial/ethnic/cultural background
   - Brief family/community context

2. SPECIFIC INCIDENTS (2-4 paragraphs, most important section)
   For each incident, describe:
   - When and where it happened
   - What specifically occurred (denied loan, lost opportunity,
     treated differently, excluded, disparaged)
   - Who was involved (don't need names, but role/institution)
   - How you know it was due to your identity
   - What the material impact was (lost money, lost opportunity,
     delayed business growth, emotional/professional harm)

3. PATTERN OF BARRIERS (1-2 paragraphs)
   - How these experiences created ongoing barriers to
     business entry or advancement
   - Specific ways discrimination affected your access to
     capital, contracts, networks, or opportunities

4. IMPACT ON BUSINESS (1 paragraph)
   - How social disadvantage has specifically impacted
     Adverse LLC's ability to compete fairly
   - Why the 8(a) program would help level the playing field
```

**Tips:**
- Be specific. "I was denied a loan" is weak. "In March 2023, I applied for a $50K business line of credit at [Bank]. Despite meeting all financial requirements, my application was denied. I later learned through a banker contact that similarly qualified white applicants were approved. This forced me to self-fund operations, limiting my growth capacity for 8 months." — that's strong.
- Include 2-4 incidents minimum
- Show MATERIAL HARM — not just hurt feelings, but financial/professional impact
- Keep it factual and professional in tone
- Length: 2-5 pages is typical
- Supporting evidence (emails, denial letters, records) strengthens the case but isn't always required

## FAQ

**Q: Can I start Move 2 and 3 before Move 1 is complete?**
A: You can start SAM.gov registration (needed for 8(a)) in parallel with HUB. But CBI MBE registration requires HUB first. You can apply for CBI SBE without HUB, then add MBE later.

**Q: What if Adverse LLC is less than 2 years old for 8(a)?**
A: You need either 2 years of business operations OR equivalent management/technical experience. If Adam has 2+ years of professional experience in digital services (even employed elsewhere), that can satisfy the requirement. The SBA evaluates "potential for success" holistically.

**Q: Should I hire a consultant for 8(a)?**
A: Not necessary but an option. SBTDC GCAP provides free guidance. If you want hand-holding on the narrative and document prep, consultants charge $3K-$10K. Given that the program unlocks millions in contract access, it can be worth it — but try the free route first (SBTDC + SCORE).

**Q: What if my social disadvantage narrative gets rejected?**
A: You can reapply after 12 months. The key is specificity. Vague claims of "systemic racism" won't pass. Concrete incidents with dates, parties, and documented financial impact will. If denied, the SBA provides reasons — address them in a resubmission.

**Q: Do these certifications help with my current business (selling to SMBs)?**
A: The certifications primarily help with government/enterprise sales. But the training, mentoring, and networking from SCORE, SBTDC, and CBI workshops benefit all aspects of your business. And the credibility of being "HUB Certified" and "8(a) Certified" helps with ALL sales — it signals legitimacy and vetted status.

**Q: Can I do government contracting AND keep selling to SMBs?**
A: Absolutely. Many 8(a) firms maintain both government and commercial revenue streams. In fact, the SBA encourages this — over-reliance on government contracts is a risk factor they monitor.
